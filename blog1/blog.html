Game of the Week: Hades

Hades is currently only avaliable from the Epic store, but the studio plans to move the game to Steam and other platforms once the game sees its full release.

An early access title from our friends at Supergiant Games.

Hades is not too far off from the beautifully drawn and designed games Bastion and Transistor from the same studio, but Hades attempts to jump farther into endless replay ability by using the rogue-lite system of progression that has become so popular in the last few years. The content currently in the game is top notch, but I am unsure there is enough here yet for it to be worth the money. I look forward to seeing what new content is coming, but would recommend waiting to buy until then.

Hyper Light Drifter: The Perfect Example


My standard of reviewing games is based on my love for Heart Machine's Hyper Light Drifter. I consider that game to be a perfect 10, and various aspects of the game are what I compare other games to when considering each element. For example, the soundtrack, an electronic/ orchestral piano album from longtime composer Disasterpiece, not only perfectly captures the atmosphere and mood of each scene, but also is perfectly acceptable as a stand-alone work. Without that soundtrack, the game would simply not be the same, and that is what I consider necessary for a section to be given a perfect score.
